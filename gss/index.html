Upcoming Talks

September 4 Alice Johnson — "Introduction to Perfectoid Spaces" (click for abstract)
Perfectoid spaces have transformed arithmetic geometry in the last decade. I’ll introduce the intuition behind perfectoid fields, describe why they are useful, and give some applications to understanding torsion in the cohomology of Shimura varieties.
September 11 Brian Lee — "Spectral Sequences without Tears" (click for abstract)
Spectral sequences often appear intimidating, but they’re really just a systematic way of organizing complicated homological algebra. I’ll walk through concrete examples, explain convergence, and show how they appear naturally in geometry and topology.
September 18 Clara Smith — "Stability in the Moduli of Curves" (click for abstract)
The Deligne–Mumford compactification of the moduli space of curves is one of the central constructions in algebraic geometry. I’ll give a student-friendly introduction to stable curves, the idea of adding boundary components, and some modern developments in the subject.
