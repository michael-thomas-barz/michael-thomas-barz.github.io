September 4 Igor Medvedev — "How much math did Einstein actually know?" (click for abstract)
In the 20th century Einstein formulated his famous Field Equations relating gravity to geometry, using differential geometry concepts such as curvature. At the time, these mathematical concepts were very new. On the other hand, you might have heard the myth that Einstein failed his high school math. You might have also heard that Einstein was a genius. So, how much math did Einstein actually know? In this talk, we will discuss the various mathematical ideas that go into the Einstein equations through a historical lens. Finally, we will see and try to explain some of the limitations of Einstein’s mathematical knowledge.
