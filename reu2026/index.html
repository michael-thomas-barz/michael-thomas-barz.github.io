Chicago REU 2026 Lectures

These are lecture notes for a series of 7 lectures given at the Chicago REU, in the summer of 2026, on algebraic geometry. We give a slightly non-standard introduction to the subject.
lecture-notes.pdf
Title: Calculus mod 5
Abstract:
Algebraic geometry is the study of shapes defined by systems of polynomial equations. This might seem a very strange class of shapes to single out. However, one advantage of a polynomial equation like y = x² is that it makes sense over many different number systems – you can solve it over the complex numbers, you can solve it over the integers, or you could solve it in modular arithmetic. A remarkable phenomenon in algebraic geometry is that these different incarnations of the shape somehow know about each other: you can predict the topology of the shape over the complex numbers by knowing about its incarnations over finite fields, for example. We are going to try and explore basic notions in algebraic geometry, starting with the idea of the functor of points of an algebraic variety. We will have a strong bias towards exploring how to do calculus (in the guise of ‘algebraic de Rham cohomology’) over different number systems, and how these new notions of calculus relate to calculus over the complex numbers. No background in commutative algebra or algebraic geometry will be assumed, but we will assume the audience knows what a ring is, and is comfortable with standard topics in multivariable calculus and linear algebra.

Lectures

  • Lecture 1. What is algebraic geometry?
    We try to explain how and why to study shapes defined by systems of polynomial equations.
  • Lecture 2. Hensel's lemma
    Hensel's lemma allows one to take a solution to a polynomial equation modulo a prime number p, and lift it to a solution modulo powers of p.
  • Lecture 3. Formally etale morphisms
    How do you apply Hensel's lemma to a system of polynomial equations, instead of to a single equation? Today, we start to understand that.
  • Lecture 4. High-dimensional Hensel's lemma
    We state a variant of Hensel's lemma in higher dimensions, and introduce derivations.
  • Lecture 5. Derivations
    We study derivations in more detail, and introduce Kahler differentials.
  • Lecture 6. An elliptic curve modulo p.
    After many lectures in which we explored how to lift mod p solutions of a system of polynomial equations to solutions modulo powers of p, we finally say something about how to actually find the mod p solutions to begin with!
